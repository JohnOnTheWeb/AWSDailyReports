## Trend Reflection

**Summary:** The shift from traditional AI coding assistants to agentic development environments has accelerated significantly, with major platform migrations beginning and enterprise-grade multi-agent SDLC orchestration entering production deployment. Internal Developer Platform adoption challenges persist despite substantial organizational investment and platform engineering role premiums.

**Key Deltas:**
- Amazon Q Developer's transition to Kiro marks the first major migration from reactive AI assistants to proactive, spec-driven agentic environments
- IBM Bob's enterprise rollout represents the maturation of multi-agent SDLC orchestration beyond individual coding assistance tools
- Platform engineering salary premiums reached 30-60% above generalist DevOps roles, indicating critical market demand
- IDP adoption crisis deepened with only 10% actual usage despite 80% of organizations having dedicated platform teams
- GitHub Copilot SDK enabled programmable agentic workflows, moving beyond simple prompt-response interactions
- Enterprise agentic platforms now coordinate specialized agents across entire SDLC roles rather than providing point solutions

**Velocity:** **High** - Major platform transitions and enterprise agentic SDLC launches indicate rapid evolution from the experimental agentic AI tools tracked in April 2026 to production-ready enterprise solutions, while persistent IDP adoption challenges have intensified organizational urgency around developer experience strategy.
